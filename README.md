
# SUBATOMIC SYMPHONY _ v 0 Alpha

## CONCEPT ##

IT

Subatomic Symphony è un’installazione immersiva di data visualization e data sonification, realizzata da Cristian Rizzuti, Riccardo Torresi, Biagio Di Micco e gli studenti dell’Università Roma Tre e dell’Accademia di Belle Arti di Roma.
Attraverso una combinazione di laser, video-proiezioni e suoni generativi, l opera immersiva traduce in tempo reale i dati dell’esperimento ATLAS relativi alla scoperta del bosone di Higgs, trasformando numeri ed eventi scientifici in una vera e propria sinfonia visivo-sonora. Gli eventi estratti dai dataset si materializzano su uno schermo, pensato come una metafora della sezione dell’LHC in cui avvengono le collisioni subatomiche, dando vita a una coreografia di luce, forma e suono che riflette la complessità e la bellezza della fisica delle particelle.
L’opera si configura come un’esperienza immersiva, in cui la percezione dello spettatore è coinvolta da una narrativa audiovisiva che oscilla tra precisione scientifica e sensibilità artistica, tra ordine dei dati e caos delle interpretazioni sensoriali.

EN

Subatomic Symphony is an immersive installation of data visualization and data sonification, created by Cristian Rizzuti, Riccardo Torresi, Biagio Di Micco, and students from Roma Tre University and the Rome Academy of Fine Arts.
Through a combination of lasers, video projections, and generative sounds, the immersive work translates in real time the data from the ATLAS experiment related to the discovery of the Higgs boson, transforming scientific numbers and events into a true visual and sound symphony. The events extracted from the datasets materialize on a screen, conceived as a metaphor for the section of the LHC where subatomic collisions occur, giving life to a choreography of light, shape, and sound that reflects the complexity and beauty of particle physics.
The work is conceived as an immersive experience, in which the viewer's perception is engaged by an audiovisual narrative that oscillates between scientific precision and artistic sensitivity, between the order of data and the chaos of sensory interpretations.

 


## Installation

- Clone or download this repository
- Extract the content of TDImportCache.zip and place it in SUBATOMIC_SYMPHONY_rt.toe directory/ TDImportCache.
- Open the Touchdesigner file called SUBATOMIC_SYMPHONY_rt.

You should be able to see the visuals and hear the sounds being generated by the dataset. If this does not happen try updating/reloaading the dataset.

## Updating / Re-loading the dataset 

Locate the file  sorted_data in your system and re import it or update the link in the sorted_data DAT.

### Event displayer 

<img width="2215" height="1181" alt="Screenshot 2025-08-03 174703" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/79e95f1a-e3e2-4984-961f-34083b0316d4" />


Each event is composed by 3 different particles and one Neutrino called P0, P1, P2, N. Each particle is represented on a cirle according to the sin and cos of the angles Phi and Eta:
- Phi = the angle between the z axis and the vector
- Eta= the angle between the x axis and the vector

Each event is generated subsequently one after the other and each particle inside the event is triggered randomly (for the moment). 

The size of each particle is related to the Pt of that particle.
The diameter of the circle driving the sampler points is controlled by the % of each event (last column of the dataset).

Each particle generates a sound according to the specific point of the sampler being touched by the visuals. The system uses brightness values to trigger specific notes based on the mapping and the SOP geometry used in the Sampler section.

Phi and % control also the Scale and Key being used.
